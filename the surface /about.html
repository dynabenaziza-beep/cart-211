About This Project

This project looks like a simple Y2K personality test at first, but it slowly reveals a second layer hidden under the surface. The viewer thinks they are only answering harmless questions, while the interface quietly watches their behaviour.

The Concept

Modern AI systems don’t only learn from what users type. They learn from how long you pause, which button you hover, what you avoid, and how fast you scroll. This project exaggerates that idea to make it visible.

Why It Looks Retro

The aesthetic is intentionally soft, nostalgic and “safe.” Early-2000s design feels harmless, almost cute. The contrast between the look and what the system is actually doing creates tension and makes the reveal more surprising.

Interaction Breakdown

  • The test starts simple and friendly
  • Small glitches appear after a few screens
  • Warnings slowly show “hidden activity”
  • Final page reveals the system’s real behaviour

The goal is not to scare users, but to make them more aware of the invisible data patterns modern systems capture.

The Message

People often believe AI needs large text inputs to function. In reality, behaviour itself becomes a dataset. By exposing this through a simple interactive experiment, the project invites viewers to question how “normal” websites watch them online.
