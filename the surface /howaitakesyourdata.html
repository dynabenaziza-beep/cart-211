How Ai Takes Your Data

Today many sites and apps quietly use artificial intelligence in the background. Even something that looks like a simple quiz or fun personality test can become a small data collection machine.

1. Data you share on purpose

Some information is given directly by the user. You type it, click it, or upload it because the site asks for it. This can include very basic details, but over time it still builds a clear picture of you.

  • Your name or nickname
  • Email and contact details
  • Photos, comments, or posts
  • Search terms and questions
  • Answers inside games or tests

These pieces of data help systems understand what you like, what you pay attention to, and how you present yourself online. Machine learning models can then use this to give you targeted content or recommendations.

2. Data you give without noticing

The most interesting part for this project is the information you do not realise you are sharing. AI does not only look at what you say. It also looks at how you act while you move through a page.

  • How long you stay on each screen
  • Where your mouse moves or stops
  • How quickly you scroll or click
  • The buttons you avoid or ignore
  • Time of day and type of device

These small signals can reveal mood, focus, and even confidence. When combined, they slowly turn into a behavioural profile that feels very personal, even if you never typed your full story.

Why this matters

Many AI systems run quietly in the background while we browse. They record tiny traces of behaviour that seem harmless alone, but together they become a powerful data set.

In this project the fake personality test plays with that idea. At first the viewer thinks there is no AI at all. Later the interface glitches, cracks, and finally admits that the system has been watching every small decision. It is not only reading the answers. It is learning from the behaviour that sits just below the surface.
